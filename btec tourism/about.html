ABOUT TOURISM

Tourism in Ghana has been a significant part of the country's economy and cultural
exchange for many years. Ghna offers a wide range of tourism expriences, from its rich
cultura herritage and historical sites to its stunning natural landscape and vibrant cities.

Tourism in Ghana is made of many aspect and this includes:

Culture and Heritage: Ghana has a wealth of traditional festivals celebrated
throughout the year, and these are central to tourism.Festivals like Homowo (Ga people),
Odwira (Akuapem people), and Aboakyer (Denkyira people) offer an immersive cultural experience.
These festivals often feature traditional dance, music, and rituals, making them attractive
to tourists who are keen to learn about Ghana’s history and heritage.

Tourist Site: Ghana have so many tourist site which some talks about history of Ghana,
natural life, and adventure. Example of such places is the cape coast castle, Mole National park,
The Boti Water Falls, etc.

Dance: Tourism in Ghana also talks about traditional dance in Ghana. Each ethnic group in Ghana has
it own special dance. Some of this dance talks about past history’s about some ethnic groups and this
dances are normally performed during festivals, naming ceremonies, etc. Example of these dance are Adowa
by the Akans(Ashanti’s), Borborbor dance performed by the Ewes, Apatamba performed by the Fante, etc.

Music: Tourism also involves music. Music is one of the things that help in entertaining people
across the world. Ghana is popular in playing highlife music and with this we can see music is
made up of many aspects which include highlife, hip-pop, Reggie, afro beat, etc. Music also
involves instruments which make it sweet or make people enjoy it. Example of such instruments
involves drums, guitar, flute, trumpet, etc

Artworks: Tourism in Ghana in terms of art, highlights the country’s heritage and artistic
traditions. . Visitors are drawn to Ghana’s vibrant visual arts, including traditional crafts
like kente weaving, wooden carvings, and bead-making, which can be found in markets and artisan
workshops.
